# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three audiences, one surface:

1. **Hiring managers and CTOs** evaluating for senior/lead/architect roles. They want proof of capability fast — can this person think at the systems level, lead a team, AND still build?
2. **Recruiters** screening candidates. Looking for credibility signals, domain keywords, and a portfolio that's easy to skim and pass along.
3. **Potential clients** evaluating for freelance or consulting engagements. Looking for domain fit (e-commerce, SaaS, integrations) and trust signals that this person delivers.

All three share a context: they're time-constrained, comparison-shopping against other candidates, and forming an impression in under 30 seconds.

## Product Purpose

Portfolio website positioning Junmar Jose as a multi-disciplinary tech leader — Software Architect, Solutions Architect, Technical Lead, Scrum Master, and Senior Full Stack Developer — with 9+ years building integration-heavy systems across e-commerce, SaaS, and enterprise platforms.

Success looks like: a visitor leaves thinking "this person can handle anything from architecture to hands-on code to team leadership" AND "this site itself feels premium and current — clearly built by someone who cares about craft."

## Brand Personality

**Precise, engineered, confident.**

Like a well-designed developer tool. Technical precision with authority. The voice is direct, the structure is intentional, every element earns its place. Not cold — confident. Not minimal for minimalism's sake — minimal because the thinking is clear.

## References

- **Rauno Freiberg (rauno.me)** — Minimal personal site that still feels crafted, not templated. The restraint is intentional: nothing is there by accident, and the craft shows in the details.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dev portfolios** — Cookie-cutter templates with project cards, skill percentage bars, "About Me" hero sections, Dribbble-portfolio aesthetic. The "I followed a tutorial" look.
- **Corporate/enterprise** — Blue gradients, stock photography, "synergy" language, IBM/Accenture/consulting-firm vibes. Sanitized and impersonal.
- **Overly playful/creative** — Bright multi-color palettes, emojis in UI, casual tone, indie-dev blog aesthetic. Not the register for a senior architect.
- **AI-generated landing pages** — Gradient text, hero-metric templates, identical card grids, cream/sand backgrounds, eyebrow labels on every section, em-dash-heavy copy. The obvious v0/ChatGPT output.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach** — The site itself demonstrates architectural thinking. The code structure, performance, and design decisions should be as considered as the case studies describe.
2. **Show, don't tell** — Let work artifacts, architecture decisions, and system diagrams communicate expertise. Not self-congratulatory copy or claims without evidence.
3. **Precision over decoration** — Every element earns its place. No decorative flourishes that don't serve communication. Whitespace is a deliberate choice, not leftover.
4. **Restraint with intent** — Minimal because every choice is deliberate, not because nothing was attempted. The craft shows in what's NOT there as much as what is.
5. **Technical confidence** — Communicate expertise through the quality of the artifact itself, not through adjectives about yourself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance (4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text/UI components)
- Full keyboard navigation
- Screen reader friendly (semantic HTML, ARIA where needed, meaningful alt text)
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — animations degrade gracefully to instant/crossfade
- No information conveyed by color alone
